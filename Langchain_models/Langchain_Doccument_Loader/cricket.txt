The Canvas of the Crease
The morning sun begins its slow ascent, Upon a field where hours will be spent. The dew still clings to blades of emerald green, The quietest the ground has ever been. The groundsman gives the pitch a final look, A story waiting for its opening book.

The captains meet, the coin flips in the air, A moment’s silence, tension everywhere. The fielding side emerges, ghosts in white, Or vibrant colors under floodlit light. The openers walk out with steady pace, With focused eyes and courage in their face.

The bowler starts his run, a rhythmic beat, The thumping sound of fast and heavy feet. He lets it fly, a blur of red and seam, To shatter wood or break a batsman’s dream. The willow meets the leather with a crack, A sound that sends the soaring echoes back.

It finds the gap and races to the rope, A surge of joy, a flicker of new hope. But then a spinner comes to weave his spell, With flight and turn that none can quite foretell. A subtle drift, a ball that bites the dust, Testing the batsman’s patience and his trust.

The wicketkeeper crouches, poised and still, A test of sharp reflexes and of will. The slip cordons are ready for the edge, Standing like sentries on a nervous ledge. "Howzat!" a cry goes up toward the sky, The umpire waits before he makes reply.

The finger rises—silence hits the crowd, Then comes the roar, tumultuous and loud. One man departs, his head a little low, While new legs to the center start to go. Through tea and lunch, the battle carries on, Until the shadows of the day are long.

The scoreboard ticks, a pulsing, living thing, As nervous hearts to every single cling. Ten runs to win, and only one ball left, Of every breath the stadium is bereft. The bowler charges, giving all his might, The batsman swings beneath the dying light.

The ball takes flight, a comet in the dark, And clears the fence to leave a lasting mark. The game is done, the rivals shake their hands, As cheering rises from the crowded stands. For in this sport of patience, grit, and grace, The spirit of the game finds its true place.
